Greatest Common Divisor Calculator





Euclid's Algorithm:
We can calculate any greatest common divisor (gcd) by the Euclidean Algorithm:

  1. Denote the larger of the two numbers by L and the smaller by S.
  2. Divide S into L.
  3. Let the remainder be denoted by R.
  4. Replace L by S and S by R.
  5. Repeat procedure until R=0, in which case the gcd is the last value of S.

Calculate Sum of First n Natural Numbers

Gauss' method to easily sum the first n natural numbers:
Step 1: Choose a number n.
Step 2: Add 1 to the number from Step 1.
Step 3: Multiply n by the sum from Step 2.
Step 4: Divide the product from Step 3 by 2.
This gives the sum of 1 + 2 + 3 + . . . + n.

Let's Play War!
In this game, a standard 52-card deck is split between you and the dealer. Press 'War!' to start a round; this flips a card for each player. The player with the high card wins that round. Repeat this process until no cards remain and a winner is determined. In this game, King is a high card (=13) and Ace is a low card (=1). Good luck!
